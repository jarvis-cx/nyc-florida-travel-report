## 1. ⚠️ CRITICAL: HKSAR Passport Entry Requirements (Deep Dive)

### The Bottom Line: HKSAR passport holders CANNOT use ESTA. They need a B1/B2 visa.

**Hong Kong SAR is NOT part of the US Visa Waiver Program (VWP).** The VWP covers ~40 countries (mostly European, plus Japan, South Korea, Australia, NZ, Singapore, Taiwan, Brunei, etc.). Hong Kong is explicitly NOT on this list. This has not changed recently — HK has never been on the VWP.

**What this means for your family:**
- The 4 HKSAR passport holders (1 adult + 3 children) each need a **US B1/B2 tourist visa** before travel
- This requires an **in-person interview** at the US Consulate General in Hong Kong (26 Garden Road, Central)
- **As of September 2, 2025: ALL applicants including children under 14 must attend the interview** — this is a recent change. Previously, minors under 14 could skip the interview.
- B1/B2 visas for HKSAR holders are typically issued for **10 years, multiple entry**
- Current visa fee: **HK$1,288 (US$160)** per person = ~HK$5,152 for 4 applicants

### ⏰ ACTION REQUIRED NOW
- Current wait times at HK US Consulate for B1/B2 interviews can be **4-8 weeks**
- You're ~4 weeks from Easter 2026. **If you haven't already applied, do so IMMEDIATELY.**
- Online DS-160 form → pay fee → schedule interview → attend → passport returned (usually 3-5 business days after approval)
- Apply at: https://www.ustraveldocs.com/hk/

### Documents Needed (per applicant):
- Valid HKSAR passport (6+ months validity beyond trip dates)
- DS-160 confirmation page
- Interview appointment confirmation
- Photo (5x5cm, white background)
- Evidence of ties to Hong Kong (property, employment, school enrollment for kids)
- Travel itinerary, hotel bookings, flight bookings (tentative OK)
- Financial evidence (bank statements showing sufficient funds)
- For children: birth certificates, consent letters if applicable

### Canadian Passport Holder
- **Canadians do NOT need a visa or ESTA to enter the US** (unique privilege — Canada is exempt from both)
- Just show up at the border/airport with a valid Canadian passport
- Admitted for up to **6 months** as a B-1/B-2 visitor
- No advance application needed

### Mixed-Passport Family at Immigration
- The family will likely be processed in **two streams**: the Canadian adult can use the regular line; HKSAR holders go through the visa-holders line
- Suggestion: go through together as a family unit, present all passports at the same booth
- Carry **proof of family relationship** (birth certificates, marriage certificate) — CBP officers may ask
- Have a clear, consistent story: "family holiday, NYC then Florida, 2 weeks, returning to Hong Kong"

### US-China Tensions Risk
- As of March 2026, there is **no indication** that HKSAR passport holders face additional scrutiny or restrictions specifically due to US-China tensions
- HKSAR passports are treated distinctly from PRC passports by US immigration
- However, the political climate is unpredictable. Dual nationals (if any family member also holds a PRC passport) should be aware that this could complicate things
- BN(O) passport holders: this is irrelevant unless someone in the family holds a BN(O) — the BN(O) is a UK passport type and is NOT eligible for the VWP either

## 3. Current Safety & Security

### NYC Safety (April 2026)
- **2025 was the safest year in the NYC subway since 2009** (excluding pandemic years)
- Subway crime dropped 4% vs. prior year, 14% below pre-COVID levels
- Governor Hochul committed **$77 million additional** for enhanced NYPD subway patrols in 2026
- Platform barriers installed at 74+ stations (100 by end 2025)
- **However:** perception lags reality — only 50% of New Yorkers feel safe on the subway during the day, 22% at night
- **My recommendation:** The subway is safe during daytime hours for a tourist family. Avoid after 10pm. Use Uber/Lyft for late returns. Stick to well-trafficked stations.

### NYC Practical Safety Tips
- Times Square area: busy, generally safe, but pickpocket-heavy. Keep valuables close.
- Central Park: safe during daytime. Avoid after dark.
- Midtown/Upper West Side/Upper East Side: very safe family neighborhoods
- Lower Manhattan, Brooklyn Heights: fine for daytime tourism
- Avoid: East New York, Brownsville, parts of the Bronx — you have no reason to go there

### Florida Safety (Orlando/Theme Parks)
- Orlando tourist corridor (International Drive, theme park areas) is **very safe** and heavily patrolled
- Theme parks themselves have excellent security (bag checks, metal detectors at all Disney/Universal entrances)
- Tourist areas in Florida are generally safer than local neighborhoods
- **Car safety:** Don't leave valuables visible in rental cars — smash-and-grabs happen in parking lots
- **Sun/heat:** April in Florida is hot (26-30°C). A 5-year-old can overheat quickly at a theme park. Bring sunscreen, hats, portable fan, stay hydrated.

### Political Climate
- No specific protests or political events anticipated for Easter 2026 that would affect tourist areas
- US political polarization exists but doesn't materially impact tourist safety in NYC/Orlando

### Easter at Disney/Universal — CRITICAL CROWD INFO
- **Easter 2026 falls on April 5** — earlier than usual
- **Peak spring break: March 9 – April 5, 2026** — this is one of the busiest periods of the entire year
- The absolute worst weeks: **March 16-20 and March 30 – April 5 (Easter week)**
- **After April 8, crowds drop significantly** — mid-to-late April is a sweet spot
- **Epic Universe (Universal's new park) opened 2025** — adds capacity but also draws more visitors overall. "Rising tide lifts all ships" — Disney hasn't gotten quieter.

### My Strong Recommendation
If possible, structure the trip as:
1. **NYC: ~March 28 – April 5** (Easter in NYC — crowds moderate, cherry blossoms, spring weather)

- **Bring snacks:** Theme park food is expensive ($15-20/meal for kids) and queues are long

## 6. Getting Around

### NYC
- **Subway:** Best way to get around. Buy an OMNY card or use contactless credit card. Kids under 44 inches ride free. One adult unlimited 7-day pass = US$34.
- **Uber/Lyft:** Use for late nights, outer boroughs, or when everyone's tired. Budget US$15-30/ride in Manhattan.
- **Walking:** NYC is very walkable. A family will easily walk 10-15km/day.
- **DO NOT rent a car in NYC.** Parking is US$40-80/day. Traffic is terrible. Subway + Uber is cheaper and faster.

### NYC → Florida
- **Fly.** This is not a debate. NYC to Orlando is a 2.5-hour flight, US$80-200/person on JetBlue, Spirit, or Southwest.
- Driving is 16+ hours and absolutely miserable with 3 kids.
- Book JFK/LGA → MCO (Orlando) or consider Newark (EWR) for cheaper options.

### Florida
- **Rent a car.** You absolutely need one in Florida. There's no practical public transit.
- Budget: US$50-80/day for a minivan/large SUV (you need something big for a family of 5 + luggage)
- **Book NOW for Easter period** — rental cars sell out and prices surge to US$100-150/day
- **Toll roads:** Florida is full of them. Get a **SunPass** (US$4.99 + tolls, available at Publix/Walgreens) or use the rental car's toll transponder (usually a convenience fee of US$5-10/day + tolls)
- **Driving tips:** Florida drivers are... aggressive. Stay in the right lane on I-4 (Orlando's main highway). I-4 is consistently rated one of America's most dangerous highways. Use Waze.
- **Car seats:** Florida law requires car seats for children under 6. Your 5-year-old needs a booster seat. Rent from the car company (US$10-15/day) or buy one at Walmart/Target for US$25-40 (cheaper for 2 weeks).

## 10. April/Easter Specifics

### Weather

| Location | Temp Range | Conditions | What to Pack |

### Easter Weekend (April 3-5, 2026)
- **Good Friday (April 3):** Not a US federal holiday, but many offices closed. Tourist areas fully operational.
- **Easter Sunday (April 5):** Some restaurants closed; most tourist attractions open. Church services everywhere.
- **Easter Monday (April 6):** Normal business day in US (unlike HK/UK).

### Disney/Universal Easter Crowds
- **Easter week (March 30 – April 5) = 8-10/10 crowd level.** Expect 60-90 minute waits for major rides.
- **Mitigation strategies:**
  - Buy **Lightning Lane Multi Pass** (Disney, US$30-40/person/day) — skip the worst queues
  - Buy **Universal Express Pass** (US$80-110/person/day) — worth every cent during Easter
  - **Rope drop:** Be at park gates 30-45 minutes before opening. First 60 minutes = shortest waits
  - **Stay late:** Crowds thin after 7pm. Ride the big rides at night.
  - After April 8: crowds drop to 4-5/10. MUCH more pleasant.

### Spring Break Overlap
- US spring break varies by school district but the **peak overlap is March 16 – April 5** in 2026
- After Easter Sunday (April 5), spring break is effectively over → crowds plummet
- **This is why I recommend Florida for the second week (April 6+)**
